Dal

Description

A delicious hearty vegetarian meal. A staple for Indian and Bengali households. Bengali style Masoor dal has a unqiue taste due to the use of panch phoron (Bengali Five Spice). Great side dish or meal accompanied with rice or roti.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup red masoor dal
  • 3-½ cups water
  • salt to taste
  • ½ teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 2 tablespoon mustard oil
  • 2 whole bay leaves
  • 2 whole dry red chilies
  • 1 teaspoon panch phoran
  • ½ cup sliced onion
  • 2 tablespoon chopped coriander

Directions:

  1. Wash 1 cup red lentils (masoor dal) well with water 2-3 times.
  2. Drain the water and add the dal to a pressure cooker.
  3. Note – You can also cook the dal in an instant pot (PRESSURE COOK for 8 mins on high pressure) or in a pot over stovetop (30-40 minutes on medium heat)
  4. dd 3 and ½ cups of water, salt to taste, and ½ teaspoon turmeric powder to the cooker and close the lid. Pressure cook for one whistle on high heat.
  5. Simmer the heat to low and cook for 8-10 minutes
  6. Remove the cooker from heat and let the pressure release naturally. Open the lid and whisk the dal well using a wire whisk.
  7. Heat 2 tablespoon mustard oil in a small pan.
  8. Once the oil is hot, add 2 whole bay leaves, 2 whole dry red chilies, and 1 teaspoon panch phoran and let them crackle for 4-5 seconds.
  9. Add ½ cup sliced onion and fry on medium heat until onion turns slightly browned,5-6 minutes. Keep stirring at regular intervals while frying
  10. Pour the tempering over the dal and mix well.
  11. Garnish with fresh coriander and serve hot.
